Research Interest

My research interest is focused on fashion. More specifically, I am interested in people’s relationship with their clothes, and more pressingly the wastefulness and alarming state of the current fast fashion structure and the overall fashion industry. I want to analyze patterns and motivations in consuming clothing through both quantitative and qualitative research and eventually create some sort of physical designed experience –potentially something like a flashcard style set of questions– that would spark conversations regarding clothing consumption motivations and habits. I would also aim to create a supplemental informative resource that is educational on the subject of conscious and slow clothing consumption.

Some work that has informed by research interest:

Liz Ricketts’ work regarding the “used” clothing trade in Accra Ghana. She investigates and explores the ‘mentality that accepts the exportation of waste from the global North to the global South as a solution of any kind.’

More About Me

My name is Nayeli Alejandra Pérez Torres. I was born and raised in El Paso, TX and moved to San Antonio to attend Trinity University. In addition to design, I do freelance photography and personal photography work. I enjoy observing, analyzing, and talking about both art and design. I am also interested in fashion, thrifting, new media, and how and why trends come about. Outside of art and design, I enjoy playing and watching soccer (Visca Barça!) and playing the guitar and piano.
